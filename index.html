Harriet Tubman

Freedom Fighter

¿Who was Harriet Tubman?

Harriet Tubman was an escaped enslaved woman who became a “conductor” on the Underground Railroad, leading enslaved people to freedom before the Civil War, all while carrying a bounty on her head. But she was also a nurse, a Union spy and a women's suffrage supporter.

Here's a time line of Harriet Tubman:

  • 1822: Born in Araminta Ross
  • 1844: She married a free black man named John
  • 1849: Tubman escaped to Philadelphia, only to return to Maryland to rescue her family
  • 1850: Helped guide fugitives further north into Canada and helped newly freed enslaved people find work
  • 1861: Tubman saw the Union victory as a key step toward abolishing slavery
  • 1865: After the war, he retired to the family home on property he had purchased in Auburn, New York
  • 1869: Married Nelson Davis
  • 1874: She adopted a girl named Gertie
  • 1913: Dies at the age of 91

"I freed a thousand slaves I could have freed a thousand more if only they knew they were slaves."
